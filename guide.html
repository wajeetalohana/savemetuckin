Overview

This page is a small, user-facing guideline describing the main app screens and flows. It is intentionally simple — feel free to edit and expand it for your GitHub repo or documentation site.

Home Tab — What it contains

The Home tab is the main discovery screen. Typical elements include:

  • Welcome / Quick actions — greeting and shortcuts (e.g., "Register Item").
  • Listings feed — recent items posted by users (often sorted by distance, time, or relevance).
  • Filters / Search bar — let users filter by category, price, distance, or keywords.
  • Map or Nearby results — optional: show results on a map and highlight items within a radius (e.g., 2 km).
  • Notifications / Announcements — global alerts like new features or account notices.
Typical user journey on Home:
  1. Open Home → see nearby or recent posts.
  2. Tap a post to view details (photos, description, price, expiry).
  3. From post details: message seller, save to favorites, or get directions.

My Post Tab — How to create an item

This tab lists items you personally posted and includes a button to create a new item. Steps to create an item:

1) Locating & starting
  1. Open My Post tab.
  2. Tap the + Register Item button (or Create).
2) Fill in item details
  • Title: short, descriptive (e.g., "Baby stroller — good condition").
  • Description: more details, condition, how to collect.
  • Price: numeric value (or mark as free).
  • Photos: add 1–6 images showing item clearly.
  • Location: auto-detected or manual address (used for showing nearby results).
  • Expiry / Availability: optional end date or "available until picked up".
3) Submit & confirm
  1. Tap Submit or Publish.
  2. App shows confirmation; item appears under My Post and in Home feed when approved (if moderation exists).
Tip: Double-check photos and description before publishing. Use clear photos and include pickup/delivery instructions to reduce back-and-forth messages.

Sending a message to any post

Messaging is private and tied to the post. Basic flow:

From Home or Post details:
  1. Open the post you are interested in.
  2. Tap Message (or Contact).
  3. Type your message — introduce yourself, reference the post title, propose a time or ask a question.
  4. Send. The other user receives a notification (push/email depending on settings).
Messaging constraints & good practices
  • Be polite and include the post title in your first message (helps seller manage replies).
  • If the app limits messages per day (e.g., 10/day) or auto-deletes messages after a time, you'll see a warning before sending.
  • Messages are stored under a private chat linked to the item.
From My Post (as poster):
  1. Open your post → view incoming messages from interested users.
  2. Tap any message to reply, set status (e.g., "Reserved"), or mark as read.

User Profile

Profile contains your personal information and account settings. Common fields & actions:

  • Display name / Photo — visible to other users in chats.
  • Email / Phone — used for login and notifications (verify email for trust).
  • Verified badge — optional verification controls posting privileges.
  • My Posts — shortcut to manage your listings.
  • Settings — notification preferences, privacy, delete account, sign out.
Editing profile
  1. Open Profile → Tap Edit.
  2. Change display name, upload photo, update contact (note: don't store passwords in plain text).
  3. Save changes — these propagate to your messages and posts.

Upgrade to Premium

Premium plans usually unlock additional features. Example premium features:

  • Priority listing or pinning to top of feed.
  • Unlimited messages or higher daily message limits.
  • Ad-free experience.
  • Access to analytics or 'who viewed my post'.
How to upgrade
  1. Go to Profile → Upgrade or Subscriptions.
  2. Choose a plan (monthly / yearly), review price and terms.
  3. Complete payment through the platform's payment flow (App Store / Google Play / web). Keep receipts for billing.
  4. After successful payment, account gets premium privileges immediately (or after a short verification process).
Important: On iOS, subscription / in-app purchase settings must be configured in App Store Connect. If you manage server-side entitlements, ensure your subscription verification logic (receipt validation) is secure.

WG Bot — what this might mean & how it could work

The phrase "WG Bot" can mean different things depending on context. Below are common interpretations and how a bot like this might integrate with your app:

  • WireGuard (wg) assistant bot: a server-side automation that manages WireGuard VPN clients (create keys, QR codes). If you mean this, it’s usually admin-facing rather than something users interact with for posts.
  • WG-gesucht bot (housing helper): a scraper & auto-messaging helper that finds housing posts and can auto-send templated messages — caution: scraping/automated messaging may violate site policies or local laws.
  • Working Group / "wg" chat bot: an assistant in group chats for moderation, quick replies, auto-FAQ or onboarding for users.
If you want a "WG Bot" feature in your app:
  1. Decide the purpose (moderation, onboarding, auto-replies, system notifications, or admin tasks).
  2. Design simple commands and scope (what it can/cannot do). Example: /help, /report 123, or automatic "new-post" push messages.
  3. Ensure transparency — label automated messages clearly (e.g., "WG Bot — automated message").
  4. Respect privacy and legal rules — do not auto-contact external services without permission; avoid spamming users.

If you meant something specific by "wg bot", say "WireGuard bot" or "WG-gesucht bot" and I can tailor the guide with concrete examples and code snippets.

Quick example text templates

Use these message templates when contacting a seller:

  • First contact: "Hi — I’m interested in your [item title] . Is it still available? I can pick up on [date/time]."
  • Follow-up: "Thanks — where should we meet? My phone: [phone number]"
  • Seller reply sample: "Yes, it’s available. I can hold it until [date]. Let me know when you’ll collect."
